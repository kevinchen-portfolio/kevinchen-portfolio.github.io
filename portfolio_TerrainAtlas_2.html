Paper Cut Terrain    Ngorongoro, Tanzania    1:700,000

Created to mimic the look of a real paper cut map, this project tried to turn a traditional stack of contour polygons into a craftily cut and paper texture work.The processes included running Focal Statistics and Contours in ArcGIS Pro. Then, it applied the paper cut map style developed by John Nelson.

An illuminated contour method developed by Kitiro Tanaka was also applied on this map.

Vintage Hillshade    Lijiang, China    1 : 300,000

This map applied the paper cut style with colour-filled polygons. A Historic Dots tool was introduced to create a more historic looking map.

The map covers Yunnan, a south-western province in China, which has a varied landscape, encompassing snow-capped mountains, rice terraces, lakes, and deep gorges.

Realistic Terrain    Colorado, United States   1 : 380,000

This map created a realistic hillshade in Colorado, United States. Using USGS Landsat 8 data, multi-spectral imageries were combined into one image. Then, it was symbolized to highlight various land features, such as land, vegetation, and water. Following a tutorial from Tom Patterson, a natural colour map was created to mimic the realistic nature earth imagery.

Realistic Terrain With Labels  Colorado, United States 1 : 380,000

This map was a continuation of the previous one, which vector data and annotations were embedded in it.
